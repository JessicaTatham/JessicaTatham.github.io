Problems Facing the Tech World

Where have all the women gone?


In a google spreadsheet created by Tracy Chou, a software engineer at Pinterest, looking at the statistics of women in software engineering, it was found that tech companies on average employ 12.35% women engineers. Looking deeper into this, companies such as Mozilla, which employ over 500 engineers, have only 8.60% of them as female. I'm not saying that this is a huge tragity, but it is definitely something to look into. With just about 50% of the people that are using this technology being women, it would make sense that women had an input in creating it. Even Pinterest, a website that is basically geared towards women, only employs just over 13% women as software engineers. I don't think this lack of female employment in the software industry is from sexist hiring, but rather from a lack of women trained in software engineering. Looking at the computer science enrollment trends of 2011, you can see that only 11% of the computer science graduates are female. If the future of technology is to reach as many people as possible, and be enjoyed by everyone, it would make sense that a diverse group of people had a hand at making it.

Some great ideas brought up in this Forbes Articel to improve the lack of women studying tech, include:

  • Creating programs that encourage women to study tech.
  • Rework the K-12 curriculum to encourage young girls to purse opportunities in tech.
  • Combat stereotypes by advertising female successes in the tech world.
With a little bit of work, the tech world will be moving towards a more diverse and equal future.
