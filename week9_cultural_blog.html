Thinking Styles

Ever wonder what your thinking style is? You can find a quiz that will test for your Gregoric Thinking Style here.

My thinking style is the Concrete Random thinking, meaning I am an experimentor. What I believe this means, is that I think about things by trying them out, usually starting with the simplest method, and disposing of thoughts that don't have purpose. This can be both an advantage and a set back. When approaching a problem, and starting with the simplest solution, I find that my code is normally quite DRY and to the point. In my case, this also means that when being presented by others with a complex solution, I often can't wrap my head around it without lots of research. By not being hung up on thoughts that don't pass my experimental thinking, I can quickly think through options for how to solve a challenge. But this also means, that I sometimes pass quickly through ideas that sometimes should be reconsidered.

When taking my thinking style into account when it comes to DBC, I hope that it will benefit me. I feel as if my biggest struggle in phase 0 of DBC has been more complex code. I will always start the solution with the most simplistic code I know, and passes a certain complexity, I have no ideas for how to solve the challenge. I'm hoping that as I learn more code and methods, I will be able to further my experimentation and not be so flustered by complex code.

Truthfully, I'm not convinced that this small quiz can sum up my thinking style in two words, but I did find that I could relate to the idea of concrete random thinking in some ways.
